The Bee Crisis

Informational Posters

School Project • 2025

Project Brief

To design and develop a set of two-panel posters that visually tell a story of either a sequence, evolution, or cycle.
The panels can contrast or enhance each other and should focus on a single theme.

  • Under the guidance of Professor R. Brian Stone

  • Timeline: 13 Weeks

  • Collaborator(s): Solo Project

  • Key skills: Storytelling, Art Direction, Graphic Design

    • Exhibited during Singapore Design Week 2025, under the theme "Envision"

    The Narrative

    I've chosen to focus on the bee crisis, highlighting the significant issue of bee population decline that has been affecting the world for some time. The posters aim to raise awareness about the vital role of bees for us and the environment, and the threats they face from human activities.

    Final Poster Set

    A SEQUENTIAL visual narrative was opted to illustrate the key factors driving bee population decline in the U.S. and Europe, and its subsequent impact on ecosystems and crops.

    Process & Iterations

    Phase 1: Secondary Research

    I conducted secondary research on the bee crisis, including understanding the issue, its causes, and its impact on the environment. I also looked into the role of bees in pollination and their importance to the ecosystem. This research helped me understand the urgency of the situation and the need for awareness.

    Phase 2: Organising Data

    The data was intepreted to determine the sequence and storytelling. This included both qualitative aspects, touching upon people's emotions, and quantitative aspects, which are more factual. To maintain clarity, an objective, micro-view as well and the macro-view was planned out. This process provides better insight on how to present the information and storytelling effectively.

    Phase 3: Synthesising Data

    Using more facts I have gathered, I visualised individual key facts from my research through sketches in forms of charts, graphs and maps to experiment how to present the information.

    Phase 4: Crafting storytelling with visuals

    The sketches were then integrated into a cohesive visual narrative, allowing better understanding of the content that goes into each poster panel.

    High Fidelity Iterations

    Iteration #1

    Vector graphics was used to create the first draft. My initial concept was for both posters to be cohesive, so I employed a bee visual to achieve this. The first half showed live bees, while the second depicted dead bees falling, symbolising the negative consequences.

    *The colours was too contrasted with the layout being too complicated.

    Iteration #2

    Here, I simplified the design by using a more more bee-themed colour palette. Inspired by National Geographic's use of real images, I referenced that and opted for more realistic images instead as it feels more impactful.

    Iteration #3

    By reducing the text and making the images more prominent, I amplified the qualitative aspect through the visuals. I explored using a half-and-half imagery technique to ensure the two panels still felt connected. The hierarchy was also improved on.

    Iteration #4

    More negative space was added to the design, content of infertilisation of crops was also added.

    Iteration #5

    I simplified the hexagon design in the first panel because it felt too cluttered. Instead, elements like the images, year, and title was separated for better readability, but the result felt too simplistic.

    Opting for a Landscape Layout

    The current layout felt too restrictive, so I decided to change the layout to a landscape format.

    Iteration #6

    Indeed, the landscape layout felt more spacious, allowing the various elements room to breathe and drawing more focus. I was also able to easily improve the visuals.

    Guest Critique

    The guest critique was very helpful in providing feedback on the design from people who have never seen my work before. I took in their suggestions and made changes to the design.

    Iteration #7

    Using the feedback from the guest critique, I made final changes to the design, mainly improving on the reading flow and improving the consistency of the style with the colours and fonts.
