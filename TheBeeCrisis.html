The Bee Crisis

Data Visualisation • Storytelling • 2026

"Design and develop a set of two-panel posters that visually tell a story of either a sequence, evolution, or cycle.”

Developed as part of the Informative Narratives module during my second year of University, this project involved translating raw complex data into structured information through 2D visualisation. I utilised graphic storytelling to create a series of visual narratives, focusing on form, colour and hierarchy to ensure the message was both legible and engaging for the target audience.

    • The Narrative

    I've chosen to focus on the bee crisis, highlighting the significant issue of bee population decline that has been affecting the world for some time. The posters aim to raise awareness about the vital role of bees for us and the environment, and the threats they face from human activities.

    Final Poster Set

    A SEQUENTIAL visual narrative was opted to illustrate the key factors driving bee population decline in the U.S. and Europe, and its subsequent impact on ecosystems and crops.

    Process & Iterations

    Idea Conceptualisation👇

    Phase 1: Secondary Research

    I conducted secondary research on the bee crisis, including understanding the issue, its causes, and its impact on the environment. I also looked into the role of bees in pollination and their importance to the ecosystem.

    Phase 2: Organising Data

    The data was interpreted to determine the sequence and storytelling. This included both qualitative aspects, touching upon people's emotions, and quantitative aspects, which are more factual.

    Phase 3: Synthesising Data

    Using more facts I have gathered, I visualised individual key facts from my research through sketches in forms of charts, graphs and maps to experiment how to present the information.

    Phase 4: Crafting Storytelling with Visuals

    The sketches were then integrated into a cohesive visual narrative, allowing better understanding of the content that goes into each poster panel.

    Hi-Fidelity Mockups & Design👇

    Iteration #1

    Vector graphics was used to create the first draft. My initial concept was for both posters to be cohesive, using a bee visual — live bees on the first panel, dead bees falling on the second. *The colours were too contrasted and the layout too complicated.

    Iteration #2

    I simplified the design using a more bee-themed colour palette. Inspired by National Geographic's use of real images, I opted for more realistic imagery as it feels more impactful.

    Iteration #3

    By reducing text and making images more prominent, I amplified the qualitative aspect through visuals. I explored using a half-and-half imagery technique to keep the two panels connected.

    Iteration #4

    More negative space was added to the design, and content about infertilisation of crops was also included.

    Iteration #5

    I simplified the hexagon design in the first panel as it felt too cluttered. Elements like the images, year, and title were separated for better readability — but the result felt too simplistic.

    Opting for a Landscape Layout

    The portrait layout felt too restrictive, so I decided to change to a landscape format to give elements more room to breathe.

    Iteration #6

    The landscape layout felt more spacious, allowing the various elements room to breathe and drawing more focus. I was also able to easily improve the visuals.

    Guest Critique

    The guest critique was very helpful in providing fresh feedback on the design from people who had never seen my work before. I took in their suggestions and made changes accordingly.

    Iteration #7

    Using feedback from the guest critique, I made final changes to the design — mainly improving the reading flow and ensuring consistency of style with colours and fonts.

    As part of Singapore Design Week 2025, My project was exhibited at the Singapore Science Park–Shopee Lobby, part of a themed exhibition titled "Envision". Singapore Design Week is one of the region's premier design festivals, organised by the DesignSingapore Council.
