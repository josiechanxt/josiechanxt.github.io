Between Past and Progress:
A walk from Chinatown to Tanjong Pagar.

Data Collection & Publication

Brief

This project was part of the “Visualising The City” module, where students are required to conduct physical fieldwork and research to gather data for a final visualisation.

My Interpretation

Singapore began as a modest trading port that drew settlers from all walks of life. Over the past 50 years, it has transformed into a vibrant modern city, making remarkable progress. This evolution inspired me to explore how this transformation could be showcased in today's Singapore. To do so, I focused on two distinct areas: Chinatown, a historic district known for its traditional shophouses, temples, and bustling markets, and Tanjong Pagar’s modern CBD district with many offices.

Objective

By journeying along a route between these two contrasting areas, the observed surroundings and building materials serve as an insightful lens to document Singapore's transition from 'Old' (traditional) to 'New' (modern).

Data Collection Method

Photography was used to collect data authentically.

How Data is collected

A photo of a building, wall texture and tile sample were taken every 20–30 steps along a route to analyse its age and materials (Ability to reflect cultural influences over time).

Book Walkthrough

My final visualisation is an interactive two-book set (Section A: Tiles Documentation, Section B: Context to the Areas, featuring tabs and coloured icons for cross-referencing Singapore’s history and modernity.
