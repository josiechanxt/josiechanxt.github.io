Research Process

[ 1: Interviews & Surveys ]

In addition to online research, interviews and surveys were conducted to understand people's experiences with vermicomposting and their reasons for liking or disliking it. The survey was sent to the plant and vermicomposting community social groups in Singapore. I also interviewed a vermicomposter, learning about the feasibility of vermicomposting in a HDB setting, it's limitations, the suitable materials for a worm bin and how she created her DIY worm bin, which are useful information that can be applied to my project.

[ 2: Trying it for myself! ]

I also got some worms (specifically the Malaysian Blue Worm species) from a nearby pet store to try composting myself. It was an interesting experience and I understood why some people found them scary (they're a bit slimy~), but eventually, I found them quite cute. Theyâ€™re harmless and just doing their job of eating, pooping, and creating compost.

[ 3: Compiling Responses ]

The survey responses were then collected, summarised into concise points and written on post-it notes on a Miro board. They were subsequently categorised into the "likes" and "dislikes" of composting to provide clearer insights into the reasons given.

[ 4: Clustering & Finding Insights ]

The "dislikes" were then clustered into different pain points, mainly indicating that while many were open to composting, there was uncertainty about how to get started, with many expressing a desire for guidance. Other concerns included space constraints, as well as issues with smell, pests, and messiness. Interestingly, during my interview, the vermicomposter mentioned that, if done correctly, the compost should have an earthy smell.

[ 5: Campaign Narrative ]

I wrote the structure of the campaign, streamlining it to ensure its objectives and solutions were clear. My idea was to use the cute "squiggles" family to guide people on how to vermicompost while making the process fun for all ages. I also aimed to leverage the community aspect to make the process more helpful and easily accessible, allowing people to share knowledge, materials, and support one another. Overall, the goal was to encourage people to lead a more eco-friendly lifestyle and reduce food waste.

[ 6: Campaign Touchpoints ]

The campaign touchpoints were also established to ensure a smooth flow and seamless connection between them. I primarily implemented it through physical means, such as distributing kits and placing posters, as well as digital platforms like an app and website. This approach made the campaign well-rounded, offering a fun and interactive physical experience while providing easy access to information online.
