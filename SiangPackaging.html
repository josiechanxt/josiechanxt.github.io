Siang Pure Repackaging

Branding, packaging design

School Project • 2020

Project Brief

We are required to identify a significant item within our morning routine and package the product that can help us convey our selected brand, focus on its use and significance.

  • Under the guidance of Yanzo Fang

  • Timeline: 7 Weeks

  • Collaborator(s): Solo Project

  • Key skills: Branding, Graphic Design, Packaging design

    • My Concept

    A series of 3 packaging designs have to be created for our selected item to further enhance its use. In my case, I selected a Siang pure inhaler which I use every morning to refresh myself before leaving the house.

    Analysis of the original product

    I have been using this product for years and after much analysis, I found that the original blister pack was not eco-friendly as lots of plastic is used just for 1 strip of inhaler. It also gave off a cheap feeling and I felt I could expand on that and give the product an upgrade.

    Repackaging of Siang Inhalers
    (Going Premium, eco-friendly)

    The new concept offers a premium take on Siang Pure with tea-scented flavours—Rose, Green, and Peppermint—chosen for their refreshing aromas that awaken the senses. Since tea is often consumed to help people feel refreshed and awake, I incorporated this idea into the inhalers, as both share the same concept of rejuvenation. The eco-friendly packaging is designed to be attractive and is primarily made of paper.
