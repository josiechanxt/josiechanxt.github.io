GoKakis

Product Design Service Design Motion Graphics 2025

"How might we transform the rehab waiting experience to be socially and physically engaging while keeping it simple and independent?”

We were tasked to transform a raw, biomedical engineering proof-of-concept into a mature, market-ready product integrated within a broader service ecosystem. Design complexity was managed by decomposing technical constraints and reconfiguring component layouts, while simultaneously using user research to inform our process. The final project showcases the tangible value of human-centred design in turning complex utility into a seamless, accessible service.

    • The Scenario

    Long waits, disengagement, and boredom. For many elderly individuals, this is a familiar routine at rehabilitation centres as they wait for their appointments.

    The Original Product

    Originally, as a biomedical engineering project, the creators reimagined waiting by weaving physiotherapy elements into a playful hybrid gaming station of bingo and tic-tac-toe.

    How it works: User cycles with pedal (leg workout) → Ball is dispensed → User picks up ball (stretches back) → puts ball into Tic Tac Toe slot (cognitive)

    Identified Opportunities

    The original project's success proved the value of gamified physiotherapy. So we leveraged that, making the games more fun and intuitive while minimising reliance on staff to increase spontaneity.

    - Reduce staff reliance
    - Gamifying physiotherapy

    Revamped Product: GoKakis Interactive Projector

    GoKakis is an interactive projection system with an old-school charm that turns waiting times into opportunities for social interaction and fun, while gamifying physiotherapy. All it takes is an A4-sized projector, designed to resemble a traditional rattan basket, to transform any floor into a playground of memories inspired by Singapore's nostalgic past. With this solution, we aim to reduce staff reliance and foster spontaneity in play.

    Waiting together, socialising together...

    Beyond the technology, we provide a humanizing experience. Our games are inspired by nostalgic Singaporean pastimes like Longkang Fishing and Chapteh, allowing seniors to relive their past. By combining these familiar movements with physical therapy, we create a more engaging rehabilitation process and a highly social experience.

    Highly scalable, anytime anywhere in any rehab centre.
