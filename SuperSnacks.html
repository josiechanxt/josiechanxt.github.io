SuperSnacks Revamp

Service Design, User Experience

School Project • 2024

Project Brief

In this Human-Centred Design module, we were tasked with improving the service design and user experience of an F&B outlet in NUS. SuperSnacks was chosen, and our focus was to identify pain points and propose solutions to enhance operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.

Background of SuperSnacks

Improving Customer's User Journey

User Research

We started by researching the demographics of SuperSnacks' customers and found that most are campus residents who visit with the intention of having a late-night supper.

Most opt for takeaway while, using the store's ordering kiosks.

Customer Persona

Using the data from research and interviews, we crafted a persona to represent our ideal customer in order to target their needs, motivations and routines better.

Customer Experience Map

Additionally, a customer experience map was created to better understand the flow of tasks, the interaction with touchpoints with their respective pain points in the current store.

The pain points were then identified so we can improve on it to improve the customer's experience for our revamp.

Pain Point 1 : Kiosk interface

Zooming in, we examined each touchpoint individually, focusing on the customer's needs and pain points as mentioned from the interviews and surveys.

We found that the kiosk and the store layout requires the most improvements.

The current kiosk is generally quite wordy and confusing (categories options).

On our end, we tried using the kiosks ourselves to better understand the user tasks, while noting key pages.

Pain Point 2 : Food collection process

Many customers also experienced difficulties with the food collection process, as the screen was too small, making it difficult to view the numbers from a distance or outside the store.

Pain Point 3 : Seating

Some also expressed concerns about the lack of seating and found it awkward to sit with others due to the limited space available.

How Might We?

Using the data, we crafted HMWs as actionable questions to ensure our solution solves the right problem.

Revamped Store Layout

Our new store layout provides more seating and improved visibility of queue numbers with larger TV screens placed around the area.

New Customer Journey

We improved on the touch points and came up with an improved customer journey for our persona, Ryan to have a better dining experience at SuperSnacks.

Improving Worker's User Journey

Worker Persona

Using similar methods with our customers, we crafted a persona for the worker (a part-timer).

Worker Experience Map

An experience map was also crafted detailing his routine during his shift at SuperSnacks.

Pain points and opportunities were then identified so we could improve on it.

Pain Point 1 : Food Collection

From our interviews, workers expressed concerns about the food collection process, noting that customers often collected their food without notifying them. There is also a cluttering issue of food on the small counter from uncollected orders. Additionally, the queue system posed challenges for workers as it lacked a sound notification feature.

Pain Point 2 : Kitchen Layout

As for the workers in the kitchen, paths are narrow making it cramped. They also yearn for more cooking equipment to speed up workflow.

How Might We?

Using the data, we crafted HMWs as actionable questions to ensure our solution solves the right problem.

New Worker Journey

We improved on the touch points and came up with an improved worker journey for our persona, Jun Jie to have a better experience working at SuperSnacks.

Rebranding

Additionally, we rebranded the outlet, drawing inspiration from trendy restaurants popular among young people to make it more appealing, adopting a retro diner style.
