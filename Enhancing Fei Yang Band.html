Enhancing Feel Young (飞扬) Band

Service Design Branding Community 2025

"Observing and identifying daily friction points to develop design-led improvements.”

We were tasked with identifying friction points in daily life and applying design thinking to improve them. Through acute observation of societal behaviors and routines, we developed holistic solutions to address our targeted challenges.

    Every week at 6:30 pm, a humble group of seniors congregates behind Chinatown Exit A. They gather to play instruments, sing, and dance, displaying a vibrant community spirit. While passersby might see them at dinnertime, there is a much deeper story behind this group. We spent ten weeks with the band every Saturday. We followed them, going from strangers, learning about their values of aging gracefully to being part of their community. We were deeply moved by their hospitality and openness, allowing us to collaborate closely with them on prototypes and concept iterations, ultimately incorporating a brand identity into their everyday routine that truly captures who they are.

    Initial observation of seniors' routines

    We began with a brainstorming session to shortlist everyday scenarios where design could make an impact. Our initial ideas were diverse, ranging from product design projects like improving the hygienic function of dog pee trays. Ultimately, we were drawn to the lives of the seniors, a poignant and relevant topic, as ageing is often overlooked. To identify specific opportunities, we headed to Chinatown, a place known for its vibrant heritage, and a favourite gathering spot for seniors where we could observe their routines. This fieldwork allowed us to identify authentic opportunities for improvement.

    Chatting with seniors, learning about their values and routines

    We spent time chatting with various seniors, who welcomed us with stories and life lessons. These interviews helped us understand the delicate balance they maintain between their hobbies, families, and past careers. We translated these conversations into categorised personas, using their quotes and routines to ground our design process in authentic human experience.

    Insight: A daily routine for ageing gracefully

    Our interviews revealed that 'ageing gracefully' is reflected in daily routines. Seniors maintain their dignity by staying active and finding community, which in turn allows them to step back and give their adult children the space to grow.

    The “Chinatown Band” is a community of passionate seniors.

    After one of the interviews with Mr Lok, he mentioned he liked to go listen to the senior band at 5.30pm, hence we followed him there as we were curious. It was a memorable sight, with a group of senior musicians playing instruments at Chinatown Exit A. We found this significant because, upon further observation, we saw them displaying great community spirit, such as buying food for one another. Over the following weeks, we decided to work with them to discover how design could aid them and their routines. They told us they have a name, though not known to public: “Feel Young (飞扬) Band.”

    The “Feel Young”(飞扬) Spirit.

    “We perform here every week to spread joy to other seniors, so they can join us in having fun too. Sometimes we perform in Old Folks homes or Temples too to bring them happiness.“

    - Wendy Lai (Co-Leader of Feel Young)

    As the sun sets, the band gathers. No fancy stage, just shared laughter, and a love for making people smile. Everyone contributes, in small ways that mean everything, such as buying food for each other, or simply pasting the tape on the floor before performing. They are not there for fame or money, but to bring joy to the people around them, which includes other senior or passerbys.

    The Conflict.

    Yet, sometimes joy is misunderstood. One evening, the police were called over a noise complaint. They told us it was not the first time they had come. They even tried compromising, by switching to smaller, quieter speakers, but they were told to leave.

    Their goal was just to make others happy, and that’s it.

    We interviewed Mr Poh, a Co-Leader of Feel Young, and he emotionally mentioned they only wanted to have a fun time with each other, by playing music together and sharing this passion and joy to the people around them. They felt wronged that they were treated as "noisemakers".

    Opportunity: Co-creation of Band Identity.

    As designers, we thought this presented a valuable opportunity, where we could improve their public recognition by creating a strong band identity that articulates their value. An identity that embodies their spirit, so people would know who they are and what they stand for at first glance, reducing misunderstanding.

    The individuality of each member.

    We settled on flowers, a symbol that deeply resonates wth them, which is also a metaphor for personality and a blossoming community. Each member chose a flower that reflected who they are, their personality, their character and their spirit.

    Each member also contributes their passion and talent in the band. A variation of secondary motifs were designed to complement the flower chosen.

    A bouquet of individuals.

    Together, they form a visual identity that celebrates both individual spirit and collective joy.

    Each motif holds a personal story. Together, they form a cohesive identity.

    Creating low-cost, familiar, high-impact items.

    We designed from their routine, and identified familiar and recognisable everyday objects that they already use in their performances. We translated their identity onto these items we created and kept it vintage, ensuring our design belongs naturally in their hands and environment.
